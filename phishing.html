CRITICAL

1. The Sender Domain

Always hover over the "From" name. Attackers use legitimate-sounding names but the actual email address is usually a random domain like .net instead of .com.

TECHNICAL

2. Generic Greetings

Real banks know your name. "Dear Customer" or "Dear Member" is a sign that the email was sent to thousands of people at once.

PSYCHOLOGICAL

3. Artificial Urgency

Phishing relies on fear. Words like "Immediately," "24 hours," and "Permanently Suspended" are designed to make you act before you think.

MALICIOUS

4. Hidden Hyperlinks

Before clicking, hover your mouse over any button. Look at the bottom left of your browser to see the real destination. If it doesn't match the company site, it's a trap.
